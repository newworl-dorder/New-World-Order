South Korea’s President Faces Arrest as Protests Mount: Political Turmoil Deepens Amid Failed Martial Law Bid

South Korea is engulfed in political unrest as President Yoon Suk Yeol resists arrest following his impeachment. Accused of orchestrating a failed martial law attempt to maintain power, Yoon’s actions have sparked nationwide protests. Thousands have taken to the streets, demanding justice and transparency, as tensions escalate between his supporters and critics. The arrest warrant, issued amidst allegations of abuse of power and corruption, is set to expire soon, adding urgency to the unfolding crisis. Political analysts warn that the prolonged standoff could destabilize the nation, erode public trust, and impact South Korea’s economy. Meanwhile, opposition leaders call for immediate action to uphold the rule of law. International observers are closely monitoring the situation, concerned about its implications for the region’s stability. As the clock ticks, South Korea faces a critical juncture in its democratic history, with potential consequences for its leadership and governance.
